AUTOTROPHIC NUTRITION

PHOTOSYNTHESIS

Autotrophic nutrition is a process in which the organism produces their food from the simple inorganic materials such as water, carbon dioxide and mineral salts in the presence of sunlight. ... They make their own food with the help of the water, solar energy, and carbon dioxide by the method of photosynthesis.

Photosynthesis meaning - Photo means "light" and synthesis means "putting together". Photosynthesis is the process through which plants produce their own food by using carbon dioxide and water in the presence of sunlight and clorophyll



FUN FACTS ABOUT PHOTOSYNTHESIS

  

* Photosynthesis is one of the reasons life exists on Earth


* During photosynthesis plants emit a light called "FLUORESCENCE".





Hetrotrophic Nutrition





A heterotroph is an organism that cannot manufacture its own food by carbon fixation and therefore derives its intake of nutrition from other sources of organic carbon, mainly plant or animal matter. They lack chlorophyll or grow in areas with not enough sunlight.Organisms that follow the heterotrophic mode of nutrition are known as heterotrophs. the types of heterotrophic plants are-



1. Parasitic plants

2. saprophytic plants

3. symbiotic plants






Parasitic Nutrition

Parasitic plants are those plants that obtain nutrition from another plant. the plant from which the parasite obtain nutrition is called the host plant .the parasitic plants slows down the growth of the host plant






1. Complete Parasite

The plant that doesn't have chlorophyll so it sucks the food from host plant through its roots called haustoria .It is called complete parasite for example cascuta





2Parsial Parasite

The plants that has green leaves and can perform photosynthesis but the mai ly absorbs water and minerals from the host plant therefore they are called partial parasite for example mistletoe






Saprophytic Nutrition

Organisms that depend on dead and decaying plants and animals for nutrition are called saprophytes.This nutrition is known as saprotrophic nutrition. Organisms like fungi produce enzyms which breaks down plant and animal matter into liquids which can be absorbed by their root-like structures called mycelium.(a vegatative part of fungus). Plants like coralroot and indian pipe absorb nutrients from the mycelium of these fungi.





Symbiotic Nutrition

Plants that show a symbiotic relationship are called symbiotic plants.Symbiosis is a mutually beneficial relation between 2 or more organisms in which food and shelter are shared by both the organisms.It is a unique relationship between parasite and host. In symbiosis both the organisms coexist and are benefitted without any harm done to either of them.




EXAMPLE- LICHENS.Here microscopic chlorophyll-containing organisms calles ALGAE and FUNGI are involved.Algae have chlorophyll and produce food through photosynthesis,while Fungi provide shelter to algae and help it to survive in harsh weather conditions.Fungi also absorb water and nutrients from soil to help algae in photosynthesis.
